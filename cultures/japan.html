Japan: Geta & Zori

History & Significance

Geta and Zori are traditional Japanese sandals. They reflect centuries of cultural etiquette, including the importance of removing shoes indoors to keep living spaces clean.

Historically, footwear in Japan also signified social status and functionality, from wooden clogs for rainy climates to more formal zori for special occasions.

Key Facts

  • Earliest known usage: approx. 8th century.
  • Symbolic: Respect for cleanliness, tradition.
  • Etiquette: Shoes are removed indoors to maintain purity of living spaces.
